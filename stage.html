the ultimate HP Probook 6550b review

Introduction

Welcome to another review of the HP laptop series. I have done a couple in the past but always older models. What I will be reviewing today is the HP Probook 6550b laptop. An older model indeed but still a good one. Not a laptop for gaming like the ROG gaming series or MSI notebooks but a standard, on the go mobile device. So without further ado let’s get started.

Description

It is a laptop. Like any other one on the 2012 market stock. And yes, by now you should already know that it is 2017 and that this laptop is very very old for computer nerds and enthusiasts alike. But that is not a reason to stop it right there and look for another. Because my personal experience with this piece of technology is better than expected. And yes, the operating system is not up to date with modern standards (windows 10) but that is everything from the ages of the dinosaurs. It actually has a very modern IO with a display port, a VGA port, 3 USB ports (so more than the new Macbook) and a good battery life that supports up to 3 hours of intensive working on projects like: managing a server, filling in accountancy bills, browsing the news for 2 hours straight and so much more room for activities outside the workspace. As a last thing that should convince you to buy this laptop is that it is relatively light for a laptop from 2012, which is pretty astonishing for such a piece of hardware.

My opinion

As mentioned before this laptop is very mobile, with its 14 inch screen and quite good flexibility. The only downside on this thing is that it is not optimal for a few high end graphical uses. But this is not a laptop for that. This is a laptop for the small businesses.

Recommendation

This is a laptop for small businesses and people who do not need more than a simple emailreader and accountingsoftware, so don’t expect you can get fast render times or fast loading times because this laptop is far from a Razer blade stealth.
